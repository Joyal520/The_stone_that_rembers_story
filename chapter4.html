CHAPTER 4

The Reset

Vihaan woke with his forehead pressed against the steering wheel.

For a few seconds, he did not remember where he was. His ears rang softly, and his mouth tasted like dust and metal. His chest felt tight, as if the air had been knocked out of him and had not fully returned.

He lifted his head slowly.

The inside of the jeep was silent.

Too silent.

The engine was off. The dashboard was dark. Only the headlights remained on, cutting two narrow tunnels of light into the desert night. Everything beyond them dissolved into blackness.

And the stone stood there.

It loomed just beyond the reach of the headlights, half caught in the light, half swallowed by shadow. The crack running through it was deep and uneven, splitting the carved symbols like broken bones. The stone leaned forward slightly, wrong in a way that made Vihaan’s stomach tighten.

This was how it had been when he crashed. Before anything else had happened.

Vihaan swallowed hard and reached for the ignition. His hands shook as he turned the key.

Nothing.

He tried again.

The engine did not even cough. The dashboard lights blinked once—weak, tired—and went out completely.

A slow, crawling fear settled into his chest.

He opened the door and stepped outside.

The desert air felt heavy, pressing against his skin. There was no wind. No insects. No distant sound. Even the sand beneath his shoes felt muted, as if the ground itself was listening.

The road was still there, barely visible in the headlights—but something about it was wrong. The tyre marks from his jeep were gone. Not covered. Gone.

The sand lay smooth and untouched, as if no one had ever driven here.

“That’s not possible,” he whispered.

His voice sounded too loud.

He walked a few steps away from the jeep, boots crunching softly. The sound echoed unnaturally in the stillness. He turned back.

The jeep was still there. The stone was still there. Everything else felt… removed.

He pulled his phone from his pocket. The screen stayed black. He pressed the power button again and again. Nothing.

His watch had stopped. The second hand was frozen in place.

A sound reached him.

Soft. Dragging. Footsteps.

Vihaan froze.

They circled him slowly, scraping across the sand just beyond the headlights’ reach. He could hear them clearly, but he could not see anything moving. No shapes. No shadows.

“Who’s there?” he asked.

The footsteps stopped immediately.

The silence that followed pressed down harder than before, thick and deliberate, like something waiting for him to realise he was alone.

Then the world shuddered. Not violently. Precisely.

The air rippled, like heat rising from the ground—but cold. Deeply cold. The darkness wavered. The stars above flickered and vanished for a fraction of a second, then returned to the exact same positions.

Vihaan stumbled and grabbed the jeep.

When he looked again, the desert had changed.

The road behind him was gone. Not hidden. GONE.

Beyond the headlights was nothing but endless black sand, stretching in every direction.

“No,” he whispered. “No, no, no…”

He turned and ran.

He ran away from the stone, away from the jeep, away from the place where everything had gone wrong. His feet sank into the sand. His lungs burned. His legs screamed.

When he stopped—

The jeep stood in front of him.

The stone. The same place. The same leaning angle.

“This can’t be real,” he said.

He ran again, panic tearing through him now. He kicked the sand, dragged his feet, tried to leave marks—proof that he had moved.

When he stopped, gasping—

He was back again.

The stone. The jeep. The same black silence.

Vihaan collapsed to his knees. The desert did not rush him. It did not threaten. It waited.

Patient.

The sky flickered once more. Darkness folded in on itself.

When Vihaan lifted his head, his heart nearly stopped. The dashboard clock glowed faintly.

7:38 p.m.

He looked at his watch. It was ticking again. The same time. The exact same time.

“So it resets,” he whispered. “Everything resets… except that.”

The wind rose suddenly. Sand spun around him in tight, controlled circles. Voices filled the air—not whispers now, but clear, layered speech.

“YOU BROKE THE SEAL.”

“YOU WOKE US.”

“YOU CROSSED THE LINE.”

Dark shapes formed just beyond the headlights. Tall. Thin. Their bodies twisted as if struggling to stay solid. Their faces were stretched, unfinished, wrong.

The evil spirits stepped closer.

Vihaan crawled backward until his back touched the stone. It felt unnaturally cold.

One spirit leaned close.

“We could not touch the stone. But we could touch fear.”

“We showed you. We distracted you. We let you choose.”

Understanding hit him like a blow. They had not broken the balance. They had guided him into breaking it.

The desert had not attacked him. It had responded.

The spirits slowly retreated into the sand, sinking without leaving marks. The wind died. The voices faded. The silence returned—but now it felt structured. Locked.

Vihaan stared at the leaning stone. The desert was not punishing him. It was holding him.

And it would not let him leave—until he corrected what fear had made him destroy.

The darkness thickened. Time tightened.

And somewhere beneath the sand, the desert prepared to repeat him.
